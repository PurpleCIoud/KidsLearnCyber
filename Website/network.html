CyberFirst: A Way to Help Young People Learn About Cyber Security
Cyber security is when you protect computers and data from bad people and hackers.
It is a very important skill for today, because technology is everywhere and always changing.
Cyber security experts help to keep our online stuff, devices and information safe and secure.

If you are a young person who likes cyber security and wants to learn more about it, you might like CyberFirst.
CyberFirst is a way to help young people find out their passion for tech by showing them the fast world of cyber security.
It is made by the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC), which is part of the UK government's spy and security group.

CyberFirst has a lot of things for different ages and levels, such as:

- **CyberFirst Trailblazers** and **CyberFirst Adventurers**: These are free half-day events for students in Year 7-9 (England and Wales), Year 8-10 (NI) and S2-S4 (Scotland). They let students and their families see how learning computer science can help them understand technology at work and at home better.
- **CyberFirst Defenders**, **CyberFirst Futures** and **CyberFirst Advanced**: These are free four or five-day courses for students in Year 10-12 (England and Wales), Year 11-13 (NI) and S4-S6 (Scotland). They talk about things like codes, finding clues, making programs, cyber attacks and cyber rules. They are done by good teachers at UK universities and colleges.
- **CyberFirst Girls Competition**: This is a free online competition for girls in Year 8 (England and Wales), Year 9 (NI) and S2 (Scotland). It makes teams of four to solve puzzles and quizzes about cyber security. The best teams from each place are asked to a big final where they fight for prizes and cups.
- **CyberFirst Bursary** and **CyberFirst Degree Apprenticeship**: These are money help schemes for students who want to do a degree in cyber security or something like that at a UK university. They give up to Â£4,000 per year for living costs, paid summer jobs at GCHQ or other government places, helping and career advice.

To join CyberFirst, you need to check if you can do each thing on the NCSC website.
You can also sign up online or contact the CyberFirst team by email or phone. Some things have deadlines, so make sure you don't miss them.

Some of the good things of joining CyberFirst are:

- You can learn new skills and knowledge in cyber security, which is a big and fun field.
- You can meet other students who like tech and cyber security like you.
- You can get praise and support from the NCSC, which is the UK's boss on cyber security.
- You can get chances for more education, training and work in cyber security.

Some examples of CyberFirst recent things are:

- The CyberFirst Girls Competition 2023, which happened in January and February 2023. Over 12,000 teams from all over the UK did the online round, and 13 teams got to the big final, which was online because of COVID-19 rules. The winning team was from St Paul's Girls' School in London.
- The CyberFirst Trailblazers and Adventurers courses, which runned in February and March 2023. Over 5,000 students went to these events at different places across the UK. They learned about things like cyber dangers, encryption, coding, artificial intelligence and robots.
- The CyberFirst Schools and Colleges scheme, which started in September 2022. This scheme says well done to secondary schools and colleges that do good in cyber security education. Schools and colleges can apply for bronze, silver or gold badges, depending on how much they do and achieve.
The scheme is now open for applications from schools and colleges in Greater Manchester, Northern Ireland, Scotland and Wales.

Cyber security is not only a skill, but also a way of thinking.
It needs curiosity, creativity, problem-solving and teamwork.
It can also be fun, rewarding and hard.
If you have these things and want to make a good difference in the digital world, then CyberFirst is for you.

CyberFirst is not just a way, but also a group.
By joining CyberFirst, you will be part of a network of young people who share your interests and goals.
You will also have access to mentors, experts and employers who can guide you along your cyber security way.

Cyber security is not only a work, but also a love.
It can open up many chances for you in the future, whether you want to work in government, industry or school.
You will also have the happy feeling of knowing that you are helping to protect our society from cyber dangers.

So what are you waiting for? Join CyberFirst today and find out your potential in cyber security!
